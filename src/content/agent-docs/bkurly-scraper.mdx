mechanisms:
  - id: dynamodb-dedup-check
    claim: Each product URL is pre-checked against DynamoDB by itemId before a browser page is opened; already-present items are counted as SKIPPED without issuing a network request.
    invariant: Incremental runs never re-scrape or re-upload a product already in BK_MetaData.
    evidence: "main.py:207-215, utils/utility_functions.py:92-107"
    rationale: Fast O(1) DynamoDB key-value lookup avoids spawning Playwright pages for duplicates, keeping runtime proportional to new items only.
  - id: semaphore-bounded-gather
    claim: Product scrape tasks are dispatched with asyncio.gather() but gated by asyncio.Semaphore(max_concurrent_browsers) defaulting to 5.
    invariant: At most 5 Playwright browser pages are open simultaneously.
    evidence: "main.py:25, main.py:182-188, main.py:219"
    rationale: Playwright pages are opened per-product but capped at 5 concurrent browsers to stay under anti-bot thresholds; gather() maximises throughput within that cap.
  - id: hardcoded-3s-wait
    claim: A hard-coded 3-second wait_for_timeout follows every page.goto() call on category and product pages.
    evidence: "main.py:36-37, main.py:151-152"
    rationale: Beauty Kurly pages are Next.js/React SPA — wait_until='domcontentloaded' alone is insufficient; 3 s ensures client-side hydration completes before content extraction.
  - id: rds-category-source
    claim: Category name-to-ID mapping is read from the shared RDS table category_source filtered by source_id='bk' rather than hard-coded in the scraper.
    evidence: main.py:239-241
    rationale: Shared category_source table normalizes category IDs across sibling scrapers (BK + OliveYoung) behind a source_id discriminator column.
  - id: png-normalize-on-upload
    claim: Every downloaded image is decoded by Pillow and re-encoded as PNG in memory before S3 upload, regardless of original format.
    evidence: utils/utility_functions.py:65-66
    rationale: Normalizes image format at the cost of possible size increase vs. original JPEG/WebP.
